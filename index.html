Step 1

Why Many Foreign Founders Choose Wyoming

Wyoming is a popular starting point for non-U.S. residents who want a simple, affordable U.S. LLC. It is not the only option, but it is one of the most commonly recommended for online businesses and e-commerce sellers.

Here are a few reasons why:

  • ✓
    Simple formation process. The paperwork is minimal and the state is straightforward to work with.
  • ✓
    Relatively low yearly fees. Wyoming's annual report fee is among the lowest of any U.S. state.
  • ✓
    Privacy. Wyoming does not require member or manager names to be listed in public state records.
  • ✓
    Common choice for online businesses. Many freelancers, e-commerce sellers, and remote service providers use Wyoming LLCs.
Note: Other states like Delaware or New Mexico can also work well depending on your situation. Wyoming is simply a common starting point. If you have specific tax or legal concerns, consider speaking with a professional.
Step 2

Check If Your Company Name Is Available

Before you form your LLC, you need to confirm that your preferred company name is available in Wyoming.

  • ✓
    Your name must include LLC or Limited Liability Company at the end.
  • ✓
    The name must not already be registered or too similar to an existing Wyoming business.
  • ✓
    You can search the Wyoming Secretary of State database for free.

Step 3

Choose a Registered Agent

Wyoming requires every LLC to have a registered agent — a person or company with a physical address in Wyoming who can receive official mail and legal notices on behalf of your business.

  • ✓
    The registered agent must have a physical Wyoming address (P.O. boxes are not allowed).
  • ✓
    They receive state correspondence and any legal service of process on your behalf.
  • ✓
    Most foreign founders use a professional registered agent service rather than a personal contact.

Step 4

Form the Wyoming LLC

Once you have a registered agent, you can form the LLC. The registered agent or a filing service usually submits the Articles of Organization to the Wyoming Secretary of State on your behalf.

  • ✓
    Company name — must include LLC or Limited Liability Company.
  • ✓
    Registered agent details — their Wyoming address.
  • ✓
    Mailing address — this can be your own address, even outside the U.S.
  • ✓
    State filing fee — typically around $100.

Processing is usually quick. Many filings are completed within a few business days. Once approved, you will receive your Articles of Organization — keep this document safe.

Step 5

Fill Out IRS Form SS-4

After your LLC is formed, the next step is getting an EIN (Employer Identification Number) from the IRS. An EIN is like a tax ID number for your business — you need it to open a bank account, hire employees, and file U.S. taxes.

Foreign owners generally cannot use the IRS online EIN system. Instead, you request your EIN using IRS Form SS-4. Fill this out before contacting the IRS.

Common SS-4 Fields

Field What to enter
Company name Your full LLC name including "LLC"
Responsible party Your full legal name (the owner)
Mailing address Your address (can be outside the U.S.)
Number of members Usually 1 for a single-member LLC
Reason for applying Started new business

Download Form SS-4 directly from the IRS website. Fill it out completely before moving to the next step.

Step 6

Get Your EIN From the IRS

You have a few options for submitting your SS-4 to the IRS. Here is a plain summary of each.

Option 1: Fax the SS-4

You can fax the completed SS-4 to the IRS. This is usually the simplest option, but processing can take several weeks. You will receive your EIN by fax or mail.

Option 2: Call the IRS Yourself

The IRS also issues EINs by phone. An IRS agent will ask the same questions shown on your SS-4. If approved during the call, you receive your EIN immediately.

Tips for the call:

  • Have your completed SS-4 in front of you
  • Spell names and addresses slowly and clearly
  • Use phonetic spelling if needed (A for Alpha, B for Bravo)
  • Be patient — wait times can be long

Step 7

File Your BOI Report

Many new U.S. companies are required to file a Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) report with FinCEN (the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network), a bureau of the U.S. Treasury Department.

  • ✓
    The filing is done through the FinCEN website — it is typically free.
  • ✓
    You will provide basic company details and information about the beneficial owners (usually you).
  • ✓
    The process is usually straightforward if your LLC structure is simple.
Important: BOI reporting rules are relatively new and details may change. Make sure to verify current deadlines and requirements directly with FinCEN or a professional. This guide is educational — not legal advice.
Step 8

Open a U.S. Business Bank Account

Once you have your EIN, you can apply for a U.S. business bank account. Several providers now accept remote applications from non-U.S. residents, which makes this step easier than it used to be.

Common documents you will likely need:

  • ✓
    Your LLC Articles of Organization
  • ✓
    Your EIN confirmation letter from the IRS
  • ✓
    A valid government-issued ID (passport)
  • ✓
    Sometimes: an Operating Agreement for your LLC

Step 9

Understand Basic Ongoing Compliance

Forming the LLC is just the beginning. There are a few ongoing requirements to keep your company in good standing.

  • ✓
    Wyoming Annual Report. Due each year. The fee is minimal (calculated based on assets in Wyoming, with a low minimum). Missing this can put your LLC out of good standing.
  • ✓
    BOI Updates. If your company details or ownership changes, you may need to update your BOI filing with FinCEN.
  • ✓
    IRS Informational Filings. Foreign-owned single-member LLCs may need to file Form 5472 and a pro forma Form 1120 with the IRS each year. This applies even if the LLC had no income. Consult a tax professional if you are unsure.
